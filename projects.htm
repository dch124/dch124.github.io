1986-1988

Knowledge based segmentation of remotely sensed images

SERC   This was a collaborative project involving Systems Designers and the Natural Environment Research Council. The goal was to develop reliable and efficient techniques for segmenting aircraft and satellite images of the land using domain specific information such as digital map data.  The project was completed successfully with the construction of a working prototype which offered a significant improvement over existing methods.
1981-1984 Matching a 3-D deformable head model to MR images  Hunnisett Fellowship   The aim of this project was to develop techniques for interpreting medical images by computer with the goal of providing automatic diagnostic assistance for a clinician. Our contribution was to apply model-based vision techniques to locate the human head from 3-D MR images.  This work was reported at the 2nd BPRA International Conference on Pattern Recognition (1983).

1978-1984

Interpretating images of a known moving object  DPhil research   Resulted in a method for locating and tracking known jointed objects from sequences of images.  The method was illustrated for the case of people walking and a program (WALKER) was constructed that successfully tracked limbs and the torso in a variety of outdoor scenes.  This work is reported in Hogg (1983), Hogg (1988) and Hogg (1989).  The WALKER program was described on Horizon ‘Science...Fiction’ and radio Science Today ‘Nerves of Silicon’.

1976-1977

Recognising human actions MSc research   Resulted in a program to produce natural language descriptions of the actions of people depicted in TV image sequences (Hogg, 1976; Hogg, 1977).
